## Technique

Sparse autoencoders are the standard tool to find interpretable features within model activations.
Yet, their architecture forces them into stratified feature bases which makes post-hoc analysis difficult.

Bilinear autoencoders are a step towards compositional autoencoders, where sums of latents are geometrically meaningful.
This allows us to decompose into complicated feature-sets, while remaining linearly analyzable.

We achieve this by linearly reconstructing the quadratic input space ($x_0^2, x_0 x_1, ..., x_n^2$),
effectively decomposing the activations into polynomial factors.
These polynomial factors can be analyzed, for instance using SVD, to find further structure within the autoencoder.
We use this toward automated extraction of subspaces that are likely to contain interesting manifolds.

Furthermore, this imposes a structural bottleneck on the model as reconstructing the quadratic input space requires $O(d^2)$ latents.
Consequently, we don't need to impose a sparsity to avoid degeneracy as we're extracting the intrinsic structure of the activations.

## Future

This work doesn't serve as a replacement for ordinary autoencoder but mainly as a proof of concept into inherently geometric autoencoders.
I don't necessarily think the _quadratic feature hypothesis_ is correct but I think it's a principled step beyond linear features hypotheses.
The hope is that this paper serves as a stepping-stone towards new methods that aren't limited by the technology of their time, and can more faithfully extract complicated features from models.
