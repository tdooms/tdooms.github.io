I've always like doing graphical design and data visualisation. 
There is something appealing about making art that is actually useful. 
Yet, I spend so little time doing this, even though I think it has good return on investment.

Personally, it feels like a waste of time to _not_ do research, it feels like procrastination.
But, as I become more senior, I'm starting to understand that good exposition is absolutely vital.
Research is not just about building atop the tower of knowledge, but also about constructing stairs.
These stairs can have many forms but I like the categorise them as: 

- **distillation**: simplifying, structuring, and relating core concepts.
- **exposition**: making ideas broadly accessible through visual or narrative means.
- **education**: transmitting high-bandwidth understanding among experts.

Clearly, there's overlap between the categories.
